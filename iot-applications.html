What is IoT Applications?


The Internet of Things (IoT) refers to a network of physical objects, devices, vehicles, buildings, and other items that are embedded with sensors, software, and connectivity, enabling them to collect and exchange data with each other and with other systems over the internet.Building IoT applications requires a combination of skills in software development, hardware engineering, and data analytics. Therefore, the essential skillsets can be: Programming skills,User interface design skills and Networking skills (Understanding of networking protocols such as TCP/IP, MQTT, and CoAP are crucial for building IoT applications that connect devices and systems over the internet).


Most popular cases of IoT Examples:

(1) Smart Homes: IoT devices such as smart thermostats, lighting systems, and home security systems can be remotely controlled via mobile devices or voice commands.

(2) Healthcare: Wearable devices, remote monitoring sensors, and medical equipment can transmit real-time data to healthcare providers, allowing for remote monitoring and patient care.

(3) Manufacturing: IoT sensors and connected devices can be used to optimize production processes, reduce downtime, and improve efficiency in manufacturing facilities.

(4) Smart Cities: IoT sensors and systems can be used to optimize city services such as traffic management, waste management, and energy consumption, leading to more sustainable and efficient urban environments.

Examples of IoT Applications:

Smart Home Security System:
A smart home security system has a lot of advantages. IoT goes well beyond conventional capabilities, enabling us to remotely operate and monitor our home via an app on our smartphone. It manages the surveillance in and around the home, along with keeping track of who has access to the doors if they are equipped with smart locks. Smart security systems gives us peace of mind while we are away.
Smart Evacuation System:
During a fire emergency, most people tend to panic and head towards a known exit that is not necessarily the best option. Therefore, a system that dynamically directs people to the safest exit is needed to reduce congestion, speed up the evacuation process and to save lives.
Hospital or Restaurant Reservation System:
The healthcare sector faces the challenge of curbing the rate at which patient's time is wasted when they visit hospitals. They are either left frustrated or tend to pay sums of monies to jump queues to receive medical care from doctors. Furthermore, this challenge is known to be caused by the manual nature of booking appointments at hospitals. In order to solve this situation, Hospital Reservation System (HRS) can help improve patient and doctor appointment bookings at hospitals. It aims to reduce and eradicate the manual process associated with hospital appointment bookings and also balance the load between a doctor and its patients.
